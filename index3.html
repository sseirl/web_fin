THE MOON

Discover the Moon

The Moon is Earth's only natural satellite and the fifth-largest satellite in the Solar System. It has a diameter of 3,474.8 km and orbits Earth at an average distance of about 384,400 km.

The Moon's surface is covered with craters formed by asteroid impacts, as well as large plains called maria. It has no atmosphere, which means there is no wind or weather, and temperatures can range from -173°C to 127°C.

The gravitational pull of the Moon causes the tides on Earth. Its phases, such as the full moon and crescent moon, are caused by the relative positions of the Earth, Moon, and Sun.

Humans first set foot on the Moon during NASA's Apollo 11 mission in 1969, with astronaut Neil Armstrong becoming the first person to walk on its surface.
